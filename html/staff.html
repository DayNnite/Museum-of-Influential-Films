Meet the Team

Our staff blends field discovery with cultural interpretation. Two “Discoverers” (Archaeology, Anthropology) and one “Interpreter” work together to preserve evidence and make meaning for visitors.

Dr. Leila Moreau

Archaeologist — Early Film Artifacts & Set Reconstructions

  • Subject Expertise: Archaeologist (film-era props, lenses, studio architecture)
  • Bio: Ph.D. in Digital Archaeology (Sorbonne). Reconstructs 19th–20th century film sets from fragmented remains using LIDAR and 3D scanning. Bridges excavation with film heritage to restore the tactile origins of cinema.
  • Museum Role: Leads Archaeology of Film Spaces; curates “Early Visionaries.”
  • Tools/Tech: LIDAR, photogrammetry, archival cataloging, conservation logging.
  • Style: Meticulous, systems-thinker; “I listen through visuals.”
  • Special Considerations: Partial hearing loss; advocates for accessible exhibit design.

Dr. Omar Delgado

Visual Anthropologist — Audience Rituals & Cultural Memory

  • Subject Expertise: Anthropologist (viewing rituals, fan cultures, communal screenings)
  • Bio: Doctorate in Anthropology (University of São Paulo). Studies how screenings become social rituals—from rural open-air shows to modern conventions—using ethnography and emotion-tracking methods.
  • Museum Role: Curates the Anthropology — Film as Reflection gallery; designs experiential displays that mirror audience reactions across eras.
  • Tools/Tech: Field interviews, qualitative coding, biometric sensors (opt-in), cultural mapping.
  • Style: Empathic, synthesis-driven, decolonial lens.
  • Special Considerations: Bilingual; integrates Indigenous South American storytelling techniques.

Dr. Celeste Imani

Cultural Historian & Mythographer — Meaning-Making Across Cinema

  • Interpretive Discipline: Cultural Historian / Mythography (symbolic analysis, semiotics)
  • Bio: D.Phil. in Cultural Semiotics (Oxford). Interprets color, gesture, and framing as modern myth; background in African & Diasporic storytelling informs cross-cultural readings.
  • Interpretive Method: Combines AI motif analysis with comparative myth to reveal recurring archetypes; collaborates with designers for interactive exhibits.
  • Museum Role: Leads narrative framing across galleries; reconciles evidence from Discoverers into public-facing stories.
  • Style: Reflective, theory-savvy, audience-centered.
  • Special Considerations: Challenges Eurocentric canons; advocates repatriation and co-authored interpretation.
